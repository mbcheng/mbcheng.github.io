THE MURAKAMI WORLD OF MUSIC
Renowned Japanese author Haruki Murakami says he owns over 10,000 records. He often references Western music artists and songs – particularly in the genres of classical, jazz, and American pop – in his stories. His first job was at a record store. Before he became an author, Murakami ran a jazz bar called the Peter Cat in Tokyo with his wife.

A WILD SHEEP CHASE
“Most everything you think you know about me is nothing more than memories”

An unnamed character is given the task to find a rare sheep that would take him on a journey from Tokyo to Hokkaido. On his way, he meets a girl with seductive ears, his old friend Rat, and stays a night at the Dolphin Hotel.

1Q84
“Even if we could turn back, we'd probably never end up where we started.”

It's 1984 in Tokyo, and Aomame has entered a paralleled world, which she calls 1Q84. Her childhood friend Tengo is an aspiring writer who takes on a ghostwriting project. Aomame and Tengo will go on a journey where their narratives will converge.

SPUTNIK SWEETHEART
“I dream. Sometimes I think that's the only right thing to do.”

K is in love with Sumire, but Sumire is attracted to an older businesswoman named Miu. One day, Sumire disappears from an island in Greece, and K goes to find her.

NORWEGIAN WOOD
“If you only read the books that everyone else is reading, you can only think what everyone else is thinking.”

Set in the late 1960s, Toru develops a romantic attraction with the emotionally troubled Midori after their best friend Kizuki died. The story revolves around the themes of coming-of-age and loss.

Haruki Murakami is a contemporary Japanese writer whose most popular books include Norwegian Wood, Kafka on the Shore, The Wind-Up Bird Chronicle, and 1Q84. Influenced by writers like Raymond Chandler, Kurt Vonnegut, Franz Fitzgerald, Raymond Carver, Murakami is often criticized by Japan's literary establishment as being un-Japanese. His stories often revolve around surrealism, magic realism, and science fiction. Murakami studied drama at Waseda University in Tokyo. He is currently 64.
