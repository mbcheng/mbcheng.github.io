Hello! My name is Michelle Cheng.

Welcome to my online space to learn more about me. I'm a New York-based journalist reporting on business, technology, and public policy–occassionally, through data-driven lens. Previously, I was on the higher education beat, leading to a my analysis on on where you go to college can impact what you study for FiveThirtyEight that was referenced in the New York Times. I've also written emoji proposals to the Unicode Consortium-seven pages advocating for the badger and swan emoji, which you can now find on your phone's keyboard. I also like to dabble in code.

I'm currently an editorial assistant and reporter at Inc. I've also written for KQED News, FiveThirtyEight, Forbes, and MIT Technology Review.

I received my undergraduate degree from Boston University, and I'm a first-generation college student. In a previous life, I was a competitive dancer.
