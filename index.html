My name is Michelle Cheng. Welcome to my space to learn more about me.

I'm a reporter for Quartz. I like to write about the societal impact of business and tech, and I am curious about the world around me.

I often take a data-driven approach to reporting. For a FiveThirtyEight story, I analyzed Department of Education data to show that most students study "practical" majors, which was later referenced in The New York Times. I've also analyzed data on commercial rents, college diversity, state funding of colleges, tech tools presidential campaigns are using, and the gender breakdown of executives leading IPOs.

My work includes reporting on a startup selling behives to Fortune 500 companies, Facebook corporate posters, the AI talent hiring frenzy, women making over the beauty industry's boys club, a gold party bus delivering donations to those impacted by Northern California fires, and the risk of employee strikes surfacing in S-1 filings.

I received my undergraduate degree from Boston University, and I'm a first-generation college student. In a previous life, I was a competitive dancer.
