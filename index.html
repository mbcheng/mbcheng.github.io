Hello! My name is Michelle Cheng.

Welcome to my portfolio and space to learn a little more about me. I'm a journalist who likes to write about people, and how business and technology impact society. Previously, I was on the higher education beat where I wrote a lot on college accessibility. I also enjoy analyzing and finding themes in data to tell compelling stories. Prior to journalism, I was a competitive dancer (I started at the age of three.) Dancing instilled in me my love of never wanting to give up on things.

I received a journalism degree and a concentration in computer science at Boston University. I'm also the first in my family to graduate from college.

I'm currently an editorial assistant and reporter at Inc. My work has been featured at KQED News, FiveThirtyEight, Forbes, and MIT Technology Review.

Fun fact: I've written emoji proposals to the Unicode Consortium.
